You always finish your task before the due date

This is an ideal situation as it allows you and your boss/client to collaborate, provide feedback, and make necessary adjustments before the final release.

You finish your task after the due date

This is a common situation where you may have to work late or over the weekend to meet the deadline. This can be stressful and may lead to burnout.

You don't never finish your task before the due date

This is the worst situation as it will likely result in a poor quality of work and a negative review from your boss/client.

How the policy works

Each project has a due week and a cutoff date. While some smaller assignments may differ, the following guidelines generally apply to large projects:

If you hand in a project during the due week:
  • It will be graded and returned to you quickly (within about ~5 business days)
  • You will receive detailed feedback on your work
  • You can resubmit portions of the assignment that received a "Does not meet requirements" on the rubric (I will provide details in the feedback)
  • A project must be at least 50% complete to be handed in during the due week
If you hand in a project after the due week but before the cutoff date:
  • It must be submitted to a different dropbox on SLATE
  • It will be graded either during reading week or at the end of the term (depending on the cutoff date)
  • You will receive a grade and a completed rubric
  • You cannot resubmit any portion of the project
If you do not submit your work by the cutoff date:
  • You will receive a grade of 0 on the project

What this policy doesn't apply to

Some projects have firm due dates where this policy doesn't apply. In these cases the standard department policy would apply. Examples include (and are not limited to):

  • In-class presentations
  • Progress meetings
  • Graded critiques
  • Graded in-class activities
  • Weekly design methods
  • Exit interviews

What if I have an accommodation/crisis/health issue/etc. ?

Just as in a job setting, you're expected to notify me promptly about any situation that may affect your work. This allows us to collaborate on creating a custom work plan tailored to your specific needs and circumstances. However, if you fail to notify me in a timely manner (for example, emailing me in the last week of class without having submitted any prior work), the aforementioned policy will remain in effect.
